For 50 years the Intercollegiate Center for Classical Studies in Rome (ICCS) has welcomed the best North American Classics students to a semester of study in Rome. The centerpiece of instruction is the Ancient City course, which has the goal of bringing the study of Classics to life by bringing our students into direct contact with the remains of Etruscan, Greek, and Roman civilization in Italy. We offer classes in Ancient Greek, Latin, Italian, and Art History as well, so that our students make good progress towards their degrees at home institutions and learn about--and enjoy--Rome and Italian culture.

The Intercollegiate Center for Classical Studies in Rome (ICCS) was established in 1965 by representatives of ten American colleges and universities. The number of member institutions has now grown to over 100. It provides undergraduate students with an opportunity in Rome to study ancient history, archaeology, Greek and Latin literature, Italian language, and ancient art.
